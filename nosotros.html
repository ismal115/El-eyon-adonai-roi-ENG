Our History

The Prophetic Missionary Ministry El Eyon Adonai Roi was officially established as a church in March 2016, emerging from God's heart through a vision given to our pastor Keisy Exela Ramírez de Marcelino. Our beginnings were marked by teaching services on Thursdays and Sundays, gradually expanding with Saturday children's Bible school and youth studies.

On December 10, 2016 we celebrated our first baptism with six brothers who gave public testimony of their faith, followed by the first foot washing and Holy Communion, thus establishing the biblical ordinances that identify us as a Pentecostal community.

Mission

Preach the Gospel in season and out of season, providing spiritual and material support to people in vulnerable conditions, especially victims of violence, following Christ's example as our model.

Vision

To be a ministry that transforms lives through the construction of the "Ray of Hope" Shelter House, forming a faith community that reflects God's Kingdom values in society.

Objectives

  • Teach fundamental biblical doctrines
  • Restore families through Christian principles
  • Provide shelter and comprehensive support to violence victims
  • Train disciples committed to Christ

Values

  • Love: Foundation of all our relationships
  • Service: Minister like Christ

Who We Are Today

El Eyon Adonai Roi is a faith family committed to biblical holiness and compassionate service. As a Pentecostal community, we live a dynamic relationship with the Holy Spirit, manifesting His gifts and fruits in our daily lives (Galatians 5:22-24).

We stand out for:

  • Genuine worship: Services where we experience God's transforming presence (John 4:23-24)
  • Solid doctrine: Teaching based on Holy Scripture as the only rule of faith (2 Timothy 3:16-17)
  • Social action: Programs for violence victims and at-risk families
  • Discipleship training: Spiritual growth processes for all ages

We continue advancing toward the construction of the "Ray of Hope" Shelter, our flagship project that will provide comprehensive shelter to women and children in vulnerable situations, combining practical help with Christ's redemptive message.

As a church, we maintain the biblical ordinances: immersion baptism, Holy Communion and foot washing, thus celebrating our identity in Christ and our Pentecostal heritage.

Since 2017, the Ministry has worked tirelessly in developing missionary and evangelistic activities with the firm purpose of bringing material and spiritual help to the most needy communities. During these years, numerous service events have been organized distributing food rations, school supplies, clothing, medicines and various essential supplies to families living in vulnerable conditions. Additionally, through medical operations and social assistance programs, care has been provided to hundreds of people without access to basic health services.


These initiatives have not only sought to meet immediate needs, but also to bring a message of faith, hope and love for others, promoting values of solidarity and compassion. Each activity has become an opportunity to sow seeds of transformation, impacting the lives of children, youth and adults in different parts of the country. Thanks to the effort of the missionary team and the collaboration of volunteers and donors, the Ministry has managed to consolidate work that transcends the material, extending a helping hand and sharing the Gospel message in every visited place.

Taking help beyond borders

From the commitment to environmental care and social responsibility, a beach cleanup was organized with the objective of preserving the natural beauty of our coasts and protecting marine life. This activity brought together volunteers of different ages who, armed with gloves, recyclable bags and great willingness, worked as a team to collect plastic waste, glass, metal and other residues affecting the ecosystem.


In addition to the cleanup, educational talks were held to raise awareness about the importance of reducing plastic use, recycling and keeping beaches free of contamination. The activity not only helped improve the natural environment but also strengthened the sense of community, fostering values of respect for the environment and care for the resources God has given us.
